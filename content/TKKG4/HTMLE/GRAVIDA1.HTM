Friday, June 16th 1961
Huge Ransom Demand in Gradiva Kidnapping


Well-informed sources tell us that Mr Gradiva, father of Eric, the kidnap victim, received a ransom demand for 100,000 pounds from the blackmailers in yesterday´s post.
No details were available about either the time or the place for the ransom handover. Last Saturday, the news of the kidnapping of little Eric Gradiva shattered the peace of our little town.

Mrs Gradiva, who was looking after her stepson at the time, told the STAR: "I was sitting with both my lovely babies in the garden. Eric was playing happily in the sand-pit. Axel was asleep in his pram. I just popped into the house to get sun-hats for the two boys and when I came back, Eric was gone! At first I though he was playing hide-and-seek, but I soon realised there was something terribly wrong." And there was - Eric had been kidnapped.

A letter from the kidnappers arrived on Monday morning, with a lock of the child´s hair. Then, just awful silence until the letter yesterday. The police haven´t a clue. Inspector Marsh said they still haven´t found any trace of the kidnappers.
